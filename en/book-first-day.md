## 🎬 First: forget what you saw in movies

The movie hacker types super fast, sees green screens, and breaks into NASA in 10 seconds. **That doesn't exist.**

Reality is calmer and more powerful:
- Cybersecurity is **90% researching, reading, and thinking**, 10% typing.
- You don't "break into" systems magically — you find mistakes someone left behind.
- It's more like being a **detective** than a wizard.
- Most of the time you're learning how something works in order to find its flaw.

If you like solving puzzles, understanding how things work, and that "aha, I get it!" feeling — this world is for you.

## 🗺️ The paths of cybersecurity

"Cybersecurity" isn't one single thing. It's a world with many specialties. Here are the main ones so you know what's out there:

- **🔴 Red Team (offensive):** you simulate being the attacker to find flaws before the bad guys do. "Pentester" (penetration tester).
- **🔵 Blue Team (defensive):** you protect, detect, and respond to attacks. You make sure no one gets in.
- **🔍 OSINT:** investigation using public information. You find everything about a target using only what's available to everyone.
- **🦠 Malware analysis:** you dissect viruses to understand how they work and stop them.
- **🔬 Digital forensics:** you're the digital CSI — you investigate what happened after an attack.
- **🌐 Web security:** you find flaws in websites and applications.
- **📡 Network / WiFi security:** you protect (or audit) networks.

**You don't have to choose now.** Try a bit of everything and see what excites you most. Most people start by exploring and then specialize.

## ⚖️ THE MOST IMPORTANT RULE (read it twice)

Before learning ANYTHING, burn this into your mind:

> **Only practice on what is YOURS or what you have PERMISSION to practice on.**

This is not optional. It's the difference between a professional and a criminal:

- ✅ **Legal:** your own computer, machines built for practice (TryHackMe, HackTheBox, DVWA), a client who hired you in writing.
- ❌ **Crime:** your school's network, a company's website, someone's account — without permission. **Even if you're "just testing".**

In the US (CFAA) and most countries there's **real jail time** for accessing systems without permission. There are teenagers with criminal records for "just being curious".

**The good news:** there are LEGAL and FREE places built for you to practice everything you want (shown below). You never need to attack anything real to learn. Ever.

Knowledge isn't the crime. **Unauthorized use** is. Be one of the good ones — they earn more, sleep well, and build careers.

## 🛠️ Set up your first lab (safe and legal)

A "lab" is your private practice ground. Here you can do whatever you want without getting in trouble. Two easy ways:

**Option A — Linux inside your Windows (recommended to start):**
- **WSL** (Windows Subsystem for Linux) — install Linux inside Windows with one command. Without deleting anything.
- Or **VirtualBox** — a "computer inside your computer" where you install Linux.

**Option B — Online platforms (zero install):**
- **TryHackMe** (tryhackme.com) — **the best place to start.** It gives you machines to hack LEGALLY, with guided step-by-step lessons. Start here TODAY.
- **HackTheBox** — harder, for when you advance.

**Your first mission:** create a free account on **TryHackMe** and do its beginner path. You'll hack your first machine (legally) on your first day. That feeling is addictive. 😎

## 📚 Where to learn (your path)

Order matters. Don't start with advanced stuff or you'll get frustrated. This is the path that works:

1. **Fundamentals first** — how **networks** and **Linux** work. Boring at first, but it's the base of EVERYTHING. (Without this, tools are magic you don't understand.)
2. **Practice on TryHackMe** from day 1 — learn by doing.
3. **Choose a path** that excites you (web, OSINT, etc.) and go deep.
4. **A programming language** (Python is ideal) — lets you create your own tools.
5. **Repeat, fail, learn.** Nobody is born knowing. Every mistake teaches you.

---

## 🚫 Beginner mistakes (avoid them)

- **Wanting to run before walking.** Don't try to "hack WiFi" on day 1. Learn the basics.
- **Collecting tools without understanding them.** Having Kali doesn't make you a hacker. Understanding WHAT each tool does, does.
- **Giving up when something doesn't work.** 90% of this is figuring out why something fails. That IS the job.
- **Practicing on others' systems "to test".** Said it already, but for its importance: NO. Use the legal labs.
- **Comparing your day 1 to someone's year 5.** We all started knowing nothing.

## 🧠 The hacker mindset

What really makes you good at this isn't memorizing commands. It's a way of **thinking**:

- **Endless curiosity:** "how does this work inside?"
- **Thinking backwards:** "what if I use this in a way it wasn't meant to be used?"
- **Not giving up:** when something doesn't work, that's the challenge, not the end.
- **Patience:** answers come to those who persist.
- **Ethics:** power is used to protect and build, not to harm.

If you have that curiosity — that need to understand how things work — you already have the most important thing. The rest is learned.

## 🆓 Free resources for your journey

You don't need to pay anything to learn this. The best resources are free:

**Platforms to practice (legal):**
- **TryHackMe** — guided paths, ideal to start. Has a free "Complete Beginner Path".
- **HackTheBox** — harder, for later. Its "Starting Point" is free.
- **picoCTF** — game-style challenges, perfect for beginners.
- **OverTheWire (Bandit)** — master the Linux terminal by playing.

**Learn theory (free):**
- **Cisco Networking Academy** — free networking courses with a certificate.
- **professormesser.com** — free CompTIA videos (Network+, Security+).
- **YouTube:** NetworkChuck, John Hammond, LiveOverflow, IppSec (HackTheBox walkthroughs).

**Certifications (the professional path):**
- **Free:** TryHackMe badges, Google Cybersecurity Certificate (scholarships), Cisco.
- **Paid (later):** CompTIA Security+ (the gateway to employment), eJPT (basic pentesting), then OSCP (the most respected in offensive).
- You don't need certs to learn — but they help land the first job.

**Communities (you're not alone):**
- Reddit: r/cybersecurity, r/hacking, r/netsec.
- TryHackMe and HackTheBox Discords.
